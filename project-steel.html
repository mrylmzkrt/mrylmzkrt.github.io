Steel Node Study

Technical Detailing | Hand Calculation

Context Steel Structure Design Course
Type Technical Drawing
Focus Construction Details
Year 2024
Tools Used AutoCAD, Profile Tables

The Concept: Constructability

Developed as part of the Steel Structure Design Course, this study focuses on the architectural detailing of steel connections, bridging the gap between design intent and physical construction. Rather than relying on automated software, the project involves a hands-on approach to calculating plate dimensions and bolt layouts based on standard steel profile data.

By manually analyzing the intersection of IPE and HEA profiles, the project demonstrates a deep understanding of construction logic and material assembly. The primary goal was to design connection plates that are not only structurally sound but also efficient for fabrication.

The resulting technical drawings showcase precise drafting skills and a commitment to understanding how building components come together, ensuring that every line on paper corresponds to a viable solution on site.
